Frequently Asked Questions

What is the FBR digital invoicing requirement?

FBR requires all sales tax-registered entities (manufacturers, importers, wholesalers, distributors, corporate and non-corporate persons) to generate digital invoices through FBR-approved software integrated with IRIS. Each invoice must include unique IRN (Invoice Reference Number), QR code, HS codes, and be submitted in real-time. As of December 31, 2025, FBR mandates digital invoicing for all businesses.

How does auto-submission to FBR work?

When you create an invoice in My Digital Invoice, our system automatically submits it to FBR's IRIS system via our licensed integrator. You instantly receive the unique IRN and QR code. All invoices are submitted in real-time (within 24 hours as required by FBR), ensuring you never miss a submission.

Who needs to be FBR compliant?

All sales tax-registered entities must comply, including: manufacturers, importers, wholesalers, distributors, retailers, service providers, and any business paying sales tax to FBR. If you have an NTN and are registered for sales tax, you need FBR-compliant digital invoicing.

What happens if I miss the deadline?

Non-compliance with FBR's digital invoicing requirements can result in penalties, fines, and issues with your tax filings. FBR has been strict about enforcement. As of December 31, 2025, digital invoicing is mandatory - start your free trial today and get compliant now.
