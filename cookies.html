Cookie Policy

This policy explains how we use cookies and similar technologies on our website.

Last updated: December 23, 2024

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to the website owners.

Cookies can be "persistent" or "session" cookies. Persistent cookies remain on your device after you close your browser, while session cookies are deleted when you close your browser.

2. How We Use Cookies

My Digital Invoice uses cookies for various purposes, including:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for the website to function properly
  • Performance Cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website
  • Functionality Cookies: Remember your preferences and settings
  • Analytics Cookies: Collect anonymous data about website usage

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Cookie Type Purpose Duration
Session Maintain your login state and preferences Until browser closes
Authentication Keep you logged in securely 30 days
Preferences Remember your settings and preferences 1 year
Analytics Understand how you use our website 2 years

4. Third-Party Cookies

We may use third-party services that place cookies on your device. These include:

  • Google Analytics: For website analytics and performance monitoring
  • Payment Processors: For secure payment processing
  • Customer Support Tools: To provide live chat and support features

These third parties have their own privacy policies governing their use of cookies.

5. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can:

  • View what cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete some or all cookies
  • Block all cookies
  • Block third-party cookies
  • Allow only certain websites to use cookies

Please note that disabling cookies may affect the functionality of our website.

6. Do Not Track

Some browsers have a "Do Not Track" feature that signals to websites that you do not want your online activity tracked. Our website does not currently respond to Do Not Track signals, but you can control tracking through the cookie management options described above.

7. Updates to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will post the updated policy on this page with a new "Last updated" date.
