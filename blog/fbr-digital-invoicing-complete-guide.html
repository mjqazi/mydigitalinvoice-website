Complete Guide to FBR Digital Invoicing in Pakistan 2025

February 3, 2026 MDI Team 10 min read

The Federal Board of Revenue (FBR) has mandated digital invoicing for all sales tax-registered businesses in Pakistan. With the December 2025 deadline approaching, it's crucial to understand the requirements and ensure your business is compliant. This comprehensive guide covers everything you need to know.

What is FBR Digital Invoicing?

FBR digital invoicing is a requirement for businesses to generate and submit electronic invoices through the FBR's Integrated Risk Information System (IRIS). Each invoice must be transmitted in real-time and includes:

  • IRN (Invoice Reference Number) - A unique identifier assigned by FBR to each invoice
  • QR Code - A scannable code that allows verification of the invoice's authenticity
  • HS Codes - Harmonized System codes for proper product classification
  • Digital Signature - Ensures the integrity and authenticity of the invoice

Who Needs to Comply?

The FBR digital invoicing mandate applies to all sales tax-registered entities, including:

  • Manufacturers
  • Importers
  • Wholesalers and distributors
  • Retailers (above the registration threshold)
  • Service providers paying sales tax
  • Corporate and non-corporate persons registered for sales tax
"As of December 31, 2025, all sales tax-registered businesses must issue digital invoices through FBR-approved software integrated with IRIS."

Key Requirements for Compliance

1. FBR-Approved Software

You must use invoicing software that has been approved by FBR and integrates with the IRIS system. My Digital Invoice is one such approved solution that handles all technical requirements automatically.

2. Real-time Submission

Invoices must be submitted to FBR in real-time at the point of sale. There's no batch processing - each invoice is transmitted immediately and receives an IRN.

3. QR Code Display

Every invoice must display a QR code that links to FBR's verification portal. Customers and authorities can scan this code to verify the invoice's authenticity.

4. HS Code Classification

All products and services must be properly classified using HS codes. This ensures correct tax treatment and helps FBR track trade activities.

Penalties for Non-Compliance

Failure to comply with FBR digital invoicing requirements can result in severe penalties:

  • PKR 500,000 - Fine for not using FBR-integrated POS
  • Business Closure - Temporary sealing of premises
  • Registration Suspension - Sales tax registration can be suspended
  • Criminal Proceedings - In cases of tax evasion
