Why bulk FBR upload is harder than it looks

FBR's IRIS is a serialized, stateful API. You can't just fire 10,000 parallel requests — it'll rate-limit you, return partial failures, and leave you with no idea which invoices submitted and which didn't. Dropped connections mean you can't just re-run the batch, or you'll double-submit.

Every Pakistani business that tried to handle this in Excel or with a generic ERP integration has hit the same wall: what do you do when invoice #4,273 of 9,000 fails and the connection drops?

Our bulk upload engine was rebuilt from the ground up to solve exactly this problem.

What makes our bulk upload different

Excel In, IRNs Out

Upload a standard Excel (.xls / .xlsx). We parse headers, validate every row against FBR's schema (NTN, HS code, tax rate, sale type), and show a clean preview before anything touches IRIS.

Two-Stage Pipeline

Validation stage first — catch bad rows before submitting. Submission stage second — only clean rows go to FBR. You never submit an invoice you haven't pre-approved.

Auto-Resume on Crash

If FBR sandbox blinks, your server restarts, or a connection drops — we pick up at the exact invoice that was pending, with no duplicates and no lost work. Every batch is checkpointed continuously.

Live Progress Tracking

Watch batches progress row-by-row in real time: validated, submitted, pending, failed. Filter failed rows, fix them in place, and re-submit without touching the Excel file again.

FBR Rate Caching

To avoid hammering FBR's rate endpoints during a 9,000-invoice run, we cache Further Tax rates by buyer registration type for 4 hours (verified) or 30 min (fallback). Bulk runs fly.

Every Row Auditable

Each uploaded invoice becomes a ledger entry with the original Excel row reference, FBR response payload, and user attribution. 10 years of retention, ready for any audit.

How a bulk batch flows

1

Drop your Excel

Any standard invoice export — from your ERP, POS, or hand-maintained sheet. We accept .xls and .xlsx, up to tens of thousands of rows per file.

2

Validation stage runs

Every row is checked for FBR schema compliance: NTN/CNIC format, HS code, sale type, tax rate, quantity, unit. Bad rows are flagged with exact error messages — nothing goes to FBR yet.

3

You review the preview

See every row, its status, and any validation issues. Fix inline or re-upload. You approve what goes to FBR before anything submits.

4

Submission stage runs

Our engine sequentially submits each validated invoice to FBR IRIS, captures the IRN and QR, and stores the response. Checkpointed continuously.

5

You get a final report

Submitted: 8,947. Failed: 53 (with reasons, re-runnable). Total time: X minutes. Every successful invoice has its IRN, QR, and audit log — ready for FBR's verification portal.
