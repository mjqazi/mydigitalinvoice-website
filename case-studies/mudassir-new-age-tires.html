The situation: one owner, three branches, every tax decision

Mudassir runs New Age Tires across three branches in Lahore — and unlike most multi-branch PK retailers, he doesn't outsource his tax work. Every FBR filing, every sales tax calculation, every audit-facing document lives on his desk. "No accountant gets it exactly how I need it," he says — so he doesn't use one.

When you self-manage three branches' FBR compliance, you can't afford gaps. Miss one invoice and the Rs 500,000 penalty doesn't care that you were busy running the other two branches.

The trigger: FBR officers walking into the showroom

The test came when FBR officers arrived at New Age Tires for an on-site audit. They didn't ask for a summary. They didn't want a quarter-end report. They picked specific invoices — individual transactions — and started asking for the supporting documents: the underlying bill, the customer paperwork, the signatures, the context.

This is the moment that reveals whether your FBR compliance is real or performative. A summary-level filing won't survive invoice-level scrutiny.

"The document-attachment feature is what makes MyDigitalInvoice indispensable for us. Every invoice has its bill, supporting documents, everything attached right to the transaction. When FBR officers came in and started questioning specific entries, I pulled up each one with a single click — invoice, attachments, audit trail, all there. They stopped asking." — Mudassir Niazi, New Age Tires, Lahore

What made the difference: attachment-grade audit trail

Most FBR invoicing tools in Pakistan treat the invoice as a submission artifact — once it's filed to IRIS, the tool's job is done. MyDigitalInvoice treats the invoice as a permanent record you'll need to defend 5 years from now:

Every invoice carries its documents. Original bill, customer signed copy, purchase order, goods-received note — attached directly to the invoice record. When FBR asks about invoice #4521 from March 2024, you don't go hunting through paper files.

Branch-level visibility. Three branches, one dashboard. Mudassir can see which branch filed what, when — and which ones haven't caught up yet.

Full user attribution. Who created the invoice, who approved it, who modified it, when. That's audit-grade data, not just a filing receipt.

10-year retention. Because FBR can look back 10 years. Your workflow needs to look forward 10 years.

What this means for other multi-branch retailers

If you run retail across multiple branches in Pakistan — or any volume operation — your FBR risk multiplies linearly. Every branch is a source of missable invoices. Every invoice is a potential audit defense.

Most tools get you filed. The good ones keep you defensible. MyDigitalInvoice was built for the second scenario, not just the first.
